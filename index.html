Strategy Explanation: Intraday Scalping V4

Core Philosophy

This strategy is designed for **scalping**â€”capitalizing on short-term intraday volatility. Success relies on finding **confluence**, where multiple technical indicators and real-time order book data align to signal a high-probability entry with a favorable risk-to-reward ratio.

Phase 1: Finding Potential Setups (Every 15 Minutes)

  • Broad Market Scan: The system scans the top 1000 Binance altcoins by volume.
  • Setup Filtering (Confluence): It identifies coins showing early reversal signs by looking for four key conditions:
    1. The price is near, or has just bounced off, the **55 EMA** on the **15-minute chart**.
    2. The price is also testing a key **Fibonacci retracement level** (0.5, 0.618, or 0.786) from the last major 15m swing.
    3. A classic **bullish RSI divergence** is present. If found on the 1m or 5m chart, the 15m RSI must be below 50.
    4. The coin shows sufficient volatility, with a **15-minute ATR of at least 0.5%** of its price.
  • Create Watchlist: Coins meeting all four criteria are added to a watchlist for continuous, real-time monitoring.

Phase 2: The Buy Trigger (Multi-Layered Confirmation)

  • Real-time Monitoring: The system scans every coin on the watchlist (every minute) for a final three-part buy trigger:
    1. Momentum Gauge: The **MACD histogram** must show increasing momentum (a "tick up") on the **3m or 5m chart**.
    2. Price Action: The most recent candle must show strong **rejection to the downside** (e.g., a long lower wick) on the **1m or 3m chart**.
    3. Order Book Filter: A sudden surge in buy-side pressure is observed (total bid volume > 2x total ask volume).
  • Execute Trade: When all three trigger conditions are met, a buy signal is sent. Only one trade can be active at a time.

Phase 3: Exiting the Trade (Dynamic & Real-Time)

  • Activate Sell Monitoring: Once a trade is active, it's monitored in real-time for an exit signal.
  • Send Sell Signal: A "close" signal is sent as soon as **one** of the following exit conditions is met (whichever comes first):
    1. The **Take-Profit** target (set to a minimum 2:1 Risk/Reward ratio) is hit.
    2. The **Trailing Stop-Loss** (based on ATR and peak price) is triggered.
    3. If the trade is in profit, a **15m MACD downtick** occurs, signaling momentum exhaustion.
    4. The hard **Stop-Loss** (initial risk level) is hit.
    5. A large **"sell wall"** appears in the order book.
    6. The price closes **below the 55 EMA** on the 15-minute chart.
  • Log and Reset: The trade is logged, and the system returns to scanning for the next setup.
