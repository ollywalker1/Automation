Strategy Explanation: Technical Confluence Long Strategy

Phase 1: Finding Potential Setups (Every 4 Hours)

  • Broad Market Scan: The system begins by scanning the top 1000 Binance altcoins (paired with USDT) by 24-hour volume.
  • Trend Filtering: It filters this list down to only include coins that are in a clear uptrend, defined by the current price being above the 55 EMA on the 4-hour chart.
  • Create Watchlist: All coins that pass this trend filter are added to a "watchlist" for constant, real-time monitoring.

Phase 2: Identifying and Ranking Buy Signals (Continuous)

  • Signal Detection: The system scans every coin on the watchlist, every minute, looking for a precise three-part buy signal:
    1. 15-Minute Chart: A classic bullish RSI divergence must be present.
    2. 5-Minute Chart: The MACD histogram must show increasing momentum (a "tick up").
    3. 5-Minute Chart: The most recent candle must be a strong bullish confirmation pattern.
  • Candidate Collection: When a coin meets these criteria, it becomes a "trade candidate" and a 60-second timer begins to allow other potential signals to be collected for comparison.
  • Confluence Scoring: After the timer, each candidate is scored out of 100 based on the quality of its setup (Divergence Strength, Momentum Surge, Volume Confirmation, and Profit Potential).
  • Select the Winner: The system selects the single candidate with the highest score. If a trade is already active, all new candidates are ignored.

Phase 3: Executing and Managing the Trade (Real-Time)

  • Send Buy Webhook: A webhook with the "create" action is sent for the single, highest-scoring coin.
  • Auto-Set Exit Targets: The system immediately calculates and sets two suggested exit points: a fixed Take Profit price (based on resistance) and a dynamic Trailing Stop % (based on volatility).
  • Activate Sell Monitoring: The trade is now "active," and the system begins monitoring it every 30 seconds for a sell signal.

Phase 4: Triggering the Exit (Whichever Comes First)

  • Send Sell Webhook: A "close" webhook is sent as soon as one of the following three conditions is met:
    1. The price hits the fixed Take Profit target.
    2. The price falls from its peak by the specified Trailing Stop percentage.
    3. A Bearish RSI Divergence is detected on the 15-minute chart, acting as a safety exit.
  • Log and Reset: The trade is logged as "closed" with the final P/L calculated, and the system returns to scanning for the next high-probability setup.
