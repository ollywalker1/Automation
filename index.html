Strategy Explanation: Technical Confluence Long Strategy

I. High Timeframe (HTF): Trend Confirmation (4-Hour)

First, we confirm the macro trend is bullish to ensure we are trading with the market's momentum. The scanner runs this check every 4 hours on all coins.

  • Indicator: 55 Exponential Moving Average (EMA).
  • Condition: Price must be trading above the 55 EMA on the 4-hour chart. Coins that meet this criterion are added to a watchlist for further monitoring.

II. Mid-Timeframe (MTF): Divergence Signal (15-Minute)

Next, for coins on the watchlist, we look for a primary signal that indicates a potential reversal and gaining internal strength.

  • Indicator: Relative Strength Index (RSI).
  • Primary Signal: The scanner looks for classic bullish divergence: price forms a lower low while the RSI forms a higher low.
  • Oversold Condition: The RSI must have dipped near or below the 30 (oversold) level during the divergence formation.

III. Low Timeframe (LTF): Entry & Confirmation (5-Minute)

Finally, we pinpoint the exact entry trigger with real-time momentum and price action confirmation. If all conditions align, a webhook is sent.

  • Momentum: The MACD Histogram must be "ticking up" (the last bar is higher than the previous one), showing accelerating positive momentum.
  • Price Action: The most recently closed 5-minute candle must be a strong bullish confirmation pattern (e.g., Hammer, Bullish Engulfing, or Piercing Line) indicating rejection of lower prices.
